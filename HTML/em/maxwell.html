Maxwell's 4 Laws of Electromagnetism

  1. Gauss' Law
  2. Unnamed Law
  3. Faraday's Law
  4. Ampere's Law with Maxwell's Addition

Each law describes fundamentally how the divergence and the curl of a field are affected by charges.

Gauss' Law says that the divergence of the electric field is directly related to the charge density enclosed. That is to say, if we draw any shape around the charge distribution, we should get an answer proportional to the charge density regardless of shape.

The Unamed Law says that the divergence of the magnetic field is always zero.

Faraday's Law says the curl of the electric field is inversely proportional to the magnetic field's change in time.

Ampere's Lawsays the curl of the magnetic field is directly proportional to the electric field's change in time, plus an referred to as the "displacement current"
