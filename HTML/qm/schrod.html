Quantum mechanics sucks and is terrible and I hate it. So, let's begin.
We have a wave function, and when we measure it, it "collapses" -- that is to say, it develops a "spike" at a particular point, such that any subsequent measurements in a short period of time will yield the same result. This seems fine as an explanation, but the math behind it is terribly complicated.
