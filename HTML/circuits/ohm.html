Ohm's Law

Ohm's law, in it's basic form, says the voltage (or, electric potential difference) across a component is equivalent to the current through the component times the resistance value of the component.

Voltage

Units: Volt [ML2T-3I-1]

All components experience an electric potential, which can be thought of as the amount of stored electrical energy. Fundamentally, a component is a wire connected to something that alters the electric flows, connected to another wire. This means that there is a different potential at either wire, since the electricity is altered as it flows across the component. The measure of this difference in electric potential is referred to as "voltage".

Current

Units: Ampere [I]

Current can be thought of as an electric analog to velocity -- it is the rate of flow of electric charge.
