Overview

An "eigenvalue" (equivalently, characteristic root) is a term used to describe the solution of a system of equations. The term "eigen" is German -- it means "same" or "own". What's the use in that description? Well, consider the motion of a rigid body, such as a wheel on a stick, unaffected by outside forces (no gravity or friction). The principal axis here is parallel to the length of the stick, centered on the center of the wheel. (Since this object is 3D there's actually 3 principal axes, but neglect the other two for now.)
